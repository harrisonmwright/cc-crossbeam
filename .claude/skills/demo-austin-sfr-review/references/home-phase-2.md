# Austin HOME Initiative — Phase II

**Ordinance:** No. 20240516-006
**Adopted:** May 16, 2024
**Effective:** May 27, 2024
**Applies to:** Applications filed on or after August 16, 2024
**Delayed applicability (November 16, 2024):** Properties in wildland-urban interface areas, or in census tracts identified as undergoing active or vulnerable to displacement by the Uprooted Report

---

## Summary

HOME Phase II focuses on **small lot single-family residential** and **flag lot** development. It enables lots as small as 1,800 sq ft within SF-3 or more restrictive districts, with significantly reduced setbacks and no building coverage limit. It also modifies how lot width and side lot line are measured, expanding the effective width of small lots.

Note: Phase II is distinct from Phase I. Phase I allowed up to 3 units on existing 5,750+ sq ft lots. Phase II enables subdivision and development of new lots as small as 1,800 sq ft for single-unit use.

---

## Small Lot Single-Family Residential Use (§25-2-779)

**Definition:** Use of a small lot for **only one dwelling unit** (not mobile homes).
**Permitted:** By right in SF-1, SF-2, and SF-3 zoning districts.

This section **supersedes base zoning district regulations** to the extent of conflict.

## Standards for SF-4A or Less Restrictive Zoning

| Standard | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Minimum lot size | 3,600 sq ft (4,500 sq ft for corner lot) |
| Maximum height | 35 ft |
| Minimum front setback | 15 ft |
| Minimum street side setback | 10 ft |
| Minimum interior side setback | 3.5 ft (combined minimum: 7 ft) — zero ft if adjacent to SF-4A zoned property |
| Minimum rear setback | 5 ft (excluding easements) |
| Rear access easement setback | 10 ft |
| Maximum building coverage | 55% |
| Maximum impervious cover | 65% |

## Standards for SF-3 or More Restrictive Zoning (Core HOME Phase II Rules)

### Lot Size and Width
| Standard | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Minimum lot size | **1,800 sq ft** |
| Maximum lot size (for this section to apply) | Less than 5,750 sq ft |
| Minimum lot width (non-flag) | **15 ft** |
| Minimum lot width (flag lot access portion) | **20 ft** |

### Setbacks
| Setback | Standard Value | Exception |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Front | **10 ft** | 5 ft for flag lots; 0 ft if lot shares front lot line with a lot subdivided <5,750 sq ft on or after Aug 16, 2024 |
| Side | **5 ft** | 0 ft if adjacent to flag lot access strip; 0 ft if shared with lot subdivided <5,750 sq ft on or after Aug 16, 2024 |
| Rear | **5 ft** | 0 ft if shared with lot subdivided <5,750 sq ft on or after Aug 16, 2024 |
| Street side (Level 1 street) | Greater of 5 ft from property line or 10 ft from curb | — |
| Street side (Level 2–4 street) | 10 ft from property line | — |

**Zero setback for attached units:** When an attached dwelling unit abuts a property line, the minimum setback for that property line is **zero** (except street side setbacks).

### Projections into Required Yards
- Windowsill, belt course, cornice, flue, chimney, eave, awning, box window, cantilevered bay window: may project **2 ft** into required yard
- One-story uncovered porch, stoop, or steps: may project **3 ft** into required yard

### Building Coverage
- **No building coverage limit** applies to property zoned SF-3 or more restrictive under this section

### Impervious Cover
- Maximum: base zoning district maximum
- Maximum front yard impervious cover (driveways/parking): **50%** (except flag lots)

### Gross Floor Area (within Subchapter F area only — §25-2-779(J))
Applies only to properties within the Subchapter F compatibility area:
- Maximum gross floor area: greater of **1,650 sq ft** or **0.55 FAR**
- Maximum unit size: **2,300 sq ft**

### Subchapter F (Residential Design and Compatibility Standards)
- **Subchapter F does NOT apply** to small lot SFR on SF-3 or more restrictive, **except** for the gross floor area/FAR provision above (§25-2-779(J))

### Design Standards (§25-2-779(K)) — non-flag lots only
- Minimum 1 street-facing entrance
- Garage placement: parking structure may not be closer to front lot line than building facade
- If garage entrance faces front yard and is <5 ft behind building facade, garage width may not exceed building facade width

### Vehicle Access Requirements (§25-2-779(L))
| Lot Width | Permitted Access |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Less than 20 ft | Alley or side street only |
| 20–29 ft | Alley, side street, or joint-use driveway with adjoining lots |
| 30 ft or more | Alley, joint-use driveway, or individual driveway |

### Short-Term Rental
- **STR prohibited** on small lot SFR (this provision expired April 1, 2025 or upon superseding ordinance)

---

## Measurement Changes

### Side Lot Line (§25-1-21)
- **New definition:** Side lot line intersects the front lot line and extends a **minimum of 25 ft** (previously 75 ft)

### Lot Width (§25-1-22(C))
- **New measurement:** Lot width is measured at the front setback line AND at **25 ft** to the rear of the front setback line (previously 50 ft)
- Effect: Tapered or irregular lots can qualify as meeting minimum width requirements more easily

---

## Flag Lots (§25-4-177)

Significant changes to allow more flag lot development:

| Condition | Minimum Flag Lot Width |
|-----------|------------------------|
| General (2+ lots sharing driveway) | 20 ft; or 15 ft if lots share driveway with utility space + alternative access or no driveway proposed |
| 1–3 dwelling units on flag lot | **10 ft** (if utility space available and no driveway proposed) |
| 1–3 dwelling units, shared driveway | **5 ft** (if driveway shared or alternative access demonstrated) |

- In SFR/duplex/two-unit/three-unit subdivisions on previously unplatted land: flag lot designs allowed for up to 2 units sharing a driveway; more than 2 units allowed if Fire Code and access requirements met
- All addresses must be displayed at closest point of access to public street for emergency responders

---

## NP (Neighborhood Plan) Combining District Modifications (§25-2-1406)

For existing legal lots platted on or before August 15, 2024, an NP combining district ordinance may:
- Reduce minimum lot area to **2,500 sq ft**
- Reduce minimum lot width to **25 ft**
- For lots ≤4,000 sq ft: increase maximum impervious coverage to **65%**

---

## Interaction with HOME Phase I

- Phase I: Allows up to 3 units on lots ≥5,750 sq ft (duplex, two-unit, three-unit)
- Phase II: Allows 1 unit on lots as small as 1,800 sq ft (small lot SFR)
- They are **complementary, not alternative**: a lot between 1,800–5,750 sq ft uses Phase II rules (1 unit); a lot ≥5,750 sq ft can use Phase I rules (up to 3 units)
- Phase II also enables subdivision of larger lots into small lots, which can then be developed under Phase I if they reach 5,750 sq ft or under Phase II if below that threshold
