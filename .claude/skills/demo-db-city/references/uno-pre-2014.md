# UNO (Pre-2014) — University Neighborhood Overlay

## At a Glance
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| LDC Section | §25-2-773 (UNO provisions, pre-2014 version) |
| Ordinance | No. 20041209-Z-11 (UNO original adoption); amended through Ord. 20130207-006 |
| Incentive Type | Density Bonus (University Neighborhood Overlay combining district) |
| Applicability | Properties within the University Neighborhood Overlay (-UNO), generally bounded by Guadalupe Street, Martin Luther King Jr. Blvd, IH-35, and Dean Keeton Street; pre-2014 version applies to projects approved before the 2014 UNO amendment effective date |

## Incentives Granted (Pre-2014 Version)
| Incentive | Standard Value | Bonus Value |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Maximum height | Base district limit (typically 40–60 ft) | Up to 60 ft with UNO Tier 1; up to 90 ft with UNO Tier 2 |
| FAR | Base district limit | Up to 4:1 (Tier 1); up to 6:1 (Tier 2) |
| Parking | Per §25-6 | Reduced: 1.0 space per unit (Tier 1); 0.5 spaces per unit (Tier 2) |
| Setbacks | Base district minimums | Front setback: 0 ft at designated street frontages |

## Affordability Requirements (Pre-2014)

**UNO Tier 1 (up to 60 ft / 4:1 FAR):**
| Parameter | Rental | Ownership |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Set-aside % | 10% of bedrooms (not units) | 10% of bedrooms |
| Max income limit | ≤65% MFI | ≤65% MFI |
| Affordability period | 40 years | 40 years |

**UNO Tier 2 (up to 90 ft / 6:1 FAR):**
| Parameter | Rental | Ownership |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Set-aside % | 15% of bedrooms (not units) | 15% of bedrooms |
| Max income limit | ≤65% MFI | ≤65% MFI |
| Affordability period | 40 years | 40 years |

**CRITICAL DISTINCTION:** Pre-2014 UNO calculates affordable housing by **bedrooms**, not by units. A 2BR unit counts as 2 bedrooms toward the set-aside.

**Math check (pre-2014):** `affordable_bedrooms / total_bedrooms ≥ 0.10` (Tier 1) or `≥ 0.15` (Tier 2)

Fee-in-lieu: Available at HCD-set rate per bedroom.

## Dimensional Standards Under Bonus
| Parameter | Tier 1 | Tier 2 |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Maximum height | 60 ft | 90 ft |
| Maximum FAR | 4:1 | 6:1 |
| Minimum front setback | 0 ft at UNO street frontage | 0 ft at UNO street frontage |
| Parking | 1.0 space/unit | 0.5 spaces/unit |

## Required Plan Elements
1. **Cover sheet:** "UNO" or "University Neighborhood Overlay" stated; LDC §25-2-773 (pre-2014) cited; ordinance reference; tier declared (Tier 1 or Tier 2)
2. **Project data table:** Total units, total bedrooms, affordable bedrooms, set-aside % of bedrooms, income limit (65% MFI), affordability period, tenure type
3. **Affordability schedule:** Each affordable bedroom (or unit containing affordable bedrooms) listed by unit ID, bedroom count, MFI tier, period
4. **Site plan:** Height labeled; FAR calculation; parking count; setbacks labeled (front setback waiver noted)
5. **Floor plans:** Affordable units/bedrooms labeled; bedroom count per unit shown
6. **Elevations:** Maximum height from natural grade; tier noted

## Key Compliance Checks
- [ ] Property within UNO combining district (zoning includes -UNO)
- [ ] LDC §25-2-773 cited (pre-2014 version)
- [ ] Tier declared (Tier 1 or Tier 2)
- [ ] Affordable calculation uses **bedrooms** (not units) — verify denominator is total bedrooms
- [ ] Affordable bedroom count ≥ 10% (Tier 1) or 15% (Tier 2) of total bedrooms
- [ ] Income limit ≤ 65% MFI (not 80% — this is stricter)
- [ ] Affordability period ≥ 40 years
- [ ] Height ≤ 60 ft (Tier 1) or ≤ 90 ft (Tier 2)
- [ ] FAR ≤ 4:1 (Tier 1) or ≤ 6:1 (Tier 2)
- [ ] Parking: 1.0/unit (Tier 1) or 0.5/unit (Tier 2)
- [ ] If fee-in-lieu: documentation present; do not compute amount
- [ ] Affordable bedrooms labeled on floor plans

## Notes
- Pre-2014 UNO is distinguished from post-2014 UNO by the bedroom-based affordability calculation and the 65% MFI income limit (post-2014 uses units and 80% MFI)
- To determine which version applies: check the project's approval date against the 2014 amendment effective date (Ord. 20140213-151, effective March 2014); projects with DSD pre-application or site plan filing before March 2014 may use pre-2014 standards
- The 65% MFI limit is unusual; ensure plans do not confuse this with the more common 60% or 80% MFI limits
- If the plans cite UNO but use unit-based (not bedroom-based) calculations and 80% MFI, the project is likely claiming post-2014 UNO standards — see `uno-post-2014.md`
